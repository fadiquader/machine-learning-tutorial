## ðŸ“Œ 1. Standard Deviation (SD)

### ðŸ”¹ What is Standard Deviation?
Standard Deviation (**SD**) measures **how spread out the data is** from the **mean (average)**.

- **Small SD** â†’ Data is **close** to the mean.
- **Large SD** â†’ Data is **spread out** from the mean.

### ðŸ”¹ Formula:
\[
\sigma = \sqrt{\frac{\sum (x - \bar{x})^2}{N}}
\]

Where:
- **Ïƒ (sigma)** = Standard deviation
- **xÌ„ (x-bar)** = Mean of the data
- **x** = Each data point
- **N** = Total number of data points
- **Î£** = Summation (sum of all values)

### ðŸ”¹ Example:
Consider the test scores: **80, 82, 85, 87, 90**

1. **Find the mean**:  
   \[
   \bar{x} = \frac{80 + 82 + 85 + 87 + 90}{5} = 84.8
   \]

2. **Find the squared differences from the mean**:

   | Score (x) | x - xÌ„ | (x - xÌ„)Â² |
   |-----------|--------|----------|
   | 80        | -4.8   | 23.04    |
   | 82        | -2.8   | 7.84     |
   | 85        | 0.2    | 0.04     |
   | 87        | 2.2    | 4.84     |
   | 90        | 5.2    | 27.04    |

3. **Find variance (ÏƒÂ²)**:
   \[
   \sigma^2 = \frac{23.04 + 7.84 + 0.04 + 4.84 + 27.04}{5} = 12.56
   \]

4. **Find Standard Deviation (Ïƒ)**:
   \[
   \sigma = \sqrt{12.56} \approx 3.54
   \]

### ðŸ”¹ Interpretation:
- Since **Ïƒ = 3.54**, the scores are **moderately spread out** from the mean (**84.8**).
- If **Ïƒ was larger**, it would mean the scores are more scattered.

## ðŸ“Œ 2. Covariance

### ðŸ”¹ What is Covariance?
Covariance measures **how two variables change together**.

- **Positive Covariance** â†’ When one variable **increases**, the other also **increases**.
- **Negative Covariance** â†’ When one variable **increases**, the other **decreases**.
- **Zero Covariance** â†’ No relationship between the variables.

### ðŸ”¹ Formula:
\[
\text{Cov}(X, Y) = \frac{\sum (X_i - \bar{X}) (Y_i - \bar{Y})}{N}
\]

Where:
- **X, Y** = Two variables
- **XÌ„, YÌ„** = Mean of X and Y
- **N** = Total number of data points

### ðŸ”¹ Example:
Consider a dataset of **Study Hours (X)** and **Exam Scores (Y)**:

| Student | Study Hours (X) | Exam Score (Y) |
|---------|---------------|---------------|
| A       | 2             | 50            |
| B       | 4             | 60            |
| C       | 6             | 70            |
| D       | 8             | 80            |
| E       | 10            | 90            |

#### **Step 1: Find Means**
\[
\bar{X} = \frac{2 + 4 + 6 + 8 + 10}{5} = 6, \quad \bar{Y} = \frac{50 + 60 + 70 + 80 + 90}{5} = 70
\]

#### **Step 2: Find (X - XÌ„) and (Y - YÌ„)**
| X | Y | (X - XÌ„) | (Y - YÌ„) | (X - XÌ„) * (Y - YÌ„) |
|---|---|---------|---------|--------------------|
| 2 | 50 | -4 | -20 | 80 |
| 4 | 60 | -2 | -10 | 20 |
| 6 | 70 | 0 | 0 | 0 |
| 8 | 80 | 2 | 10 | 20 |
| 10 | 90 | 4 | 20 | 80 |

#### **Step 3: Calculate Covariance**
\[
\text{Cov}(X, Y) = \frac{80 + 20 + 0 + 20 + 80}{5} = 40
\]

### ðŸ”¹ Interpretation:
- Covariance **40** is **positive**, meaning **study hours and exam scores increase together**.

## ðŸ“Œ 3. Correlation

### ðŸ”¹ What is Correlation?
Correlation measures **both the direction and strength** of the relationship between two variables.

- **r > 0** â†’ Positive correlation (both increase together).
- **r < 0** â†’ Negative correlation (one increases, the other decreases).
- **r = 0** â†’ No correlation.

### ðŸ”¹ Formula:
\[
r = \frac{\text{Cov}(X, Y)}{\sigma_X \cdot \sigma_Y}
\]

Where:
- **r** = Correlation coefficient (**ranges between -1 and 1**)
- **Cov(X, Y)** = Covariance of X and Y
- **Ïƒ_X, Ïƒ_Y** = Standard deviations of X and Y

### ðŸ”¹ Example:
Using previous values:
- **Cov(X, Y) = 40**
- **Ïƒ_X = 2.83**, **Ïƒ_Y = 14.14**

\[
r = \frac{40}{(2.83 \times 14.14)} = \frac{40}{40} = 1
\]

### ðŸ”¹ Interpretation:
- **r = 1** â†’ **Perfect positive correlation** (study hours and exam scores increase together exactly).

---

## ðŸ“Œ 4. Covariance vs. Correlation

| Feature       | Covariance | Correlation |
|--------------|------------|-------------|
| **Measures** | Direction of relationship | Strength & direction of relationship |
| **Range**    | Any number | Between **-1 and 1** |
| **Affected by scale?** | Yes | No (standardized) |
| **Useful for comparing different datasets?** | No | Yes |

---

## ðŸ“Œ Summary
- **Standard Deviation** measures **how spread out** data is.
- **Covariance** measures **how two variables move together**.
- **Correlation** measures **both the strength and direction** of the relationship.

import numpy as np

# Data
X = [2, 4, 6, 8, 10]
Y = [50, 60, 70, 80, 90]

# Standard Deviation
std_X = np.std(X, ddof=0)
std_Y = np.std(Y, ddof=0)

# Covariance
cov_XY = np.cov(X, Y, ddof=0)[0, 1]

# Correlation
corr_XY = np.corrcoef(X, Y)[0, 1]

print(f"Standard Deviation of X: {std_X}")
print(f"Standard Deviation of Y: {std_Y}")
print(f"Covariance: {cov_XY}")
print(f"Correlation: {corr_XY}")
